Tonkatsu

Description

Tonkatsu is a more western style dish that features breaded deep-fried pork cutlets. Although not typically eaten as a main dish, Tonkatsu can be eaten by-itself or in combination with rice or another side dish. Similar to tempura, Tonkatsu is almost always eaten with a signature dipping sauce, which will also be included in this recipe guide.

Ingredients

For the Tonkatsu:

  • 4 pork loin or pork tenderloin cutlets (about 1/2 inch thick)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • All-purpose flour, for dredging
  • 1-2 large eggs, beaten
  • Panko breadcrumbs (any breadcrumb will likely do)
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying

For Serving:

  • Shredded cabbage
  • Tonkatsu sauce (available at Japanese grocery stores or homemade)
  • Cooked Japanese short-grain rice

Instructions

  1. Prep the Pork Cutlets
    • Use a meat mallet to gently pound the pork cutlets to an even thickness, about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.
    • Season both sides of the pork with salt and pepper.
  2. Coating Process
    • Dredge each pork cutlet in flour, shaking off any excess.
    • Dip the cutlets into the beaten egg, allowing excess egg to drip off.
    • Coat the cutlets with panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere.
  3. Fry the Cutlets
    • Carefully place the coated pork cutlets into the hot oil, one at a time.
    • Fry until the tonkatsu turns golden brown and crispy on both sides, about 4-5 minutes per side. Adjust the heat as needed to maintain the temperature..
  4. Drain and Rest
    • Use a slotted spoon or a wire mesh strainer to remove the fried tonkatsu from the oil. Place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
    • Allow the tonkatsu to rest for a couple of minutes. This helps the breading set and keeps the meat juicy.
  5. Slice and Serve
    • Slice the tonkatsu into strips or pieces. Serve them on a plate alongside shredded cabbage and rice.
  6. Serve with Tonkatsu Sauce
    • Drizzle tonkatsu sauce over the tonkatsu slices or serve it on the side for dipping.
  7. Enjoy
    • Enjoy your tonkatsu with the crispiness of the breading, the tenderness of the pork, and the refreshing taste of the shredded cabbage.
